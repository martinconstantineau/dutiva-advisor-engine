# Canadian HR Compliance — Federal Knowledge Base

## Scope

This document covers core federal employment compliance obligations under the **Canada Labour Code (RSC 1985, c L-2)** and related federal instruments. It applies to federally regulated employers only.

**Federally regulated industries** (not exhaustive): banking, airlines, telecommunications, broadcasting, interprovincial transportation (rail, road, shipping), pipelines, federal Crown corporations, and nuclear facilities.

---

## Part I — Industrial Relations (Labour Relations)

**Purpose:** Governs the collective bargaining relationship between federally regulated employers and unions.

**Key provisions:**
- Right of employees to organize and bargain collectively (s. 8)
- Certification and decertification of bargaining agents via the Canada Industrial Relations Board (CIRB)
- Duty to bargain in good faith (s. 50)
- Strikes and lockouts permitted only after a lawful impasse process
- Unfair labour practices prohibited (s. 94, 95)

**Advisor note:** For any matter involving a union or collective agreement, always escalate to labour relations counsel.

---

## Part II — Occupational Health and Safety

**Purpose:** Establishes health and safety rights and obligations in federally regulated workplaces.

**Key provisions:**
- Employer's general duty to ensure workplace safety (s. 124)
- Employee's right to refuse dangerous work (s. 128)
- Right to know about workplace hazards (WHMIS, Hazard Communication)
- Internal Complaint Resolution Process (ICRP) before escalating to a safety officer
- Workplace harassment and violence: governed by the **Work Place Harassment and Violence Prevention Regulations (SOR/2020-130)**
- Serious accidents and fatalities must be reported to ESDC within 24 hours (s. 125(1)(z.16))
- Health and Safety Representative or Health and Safety Committee required (s. 135, 135.1)

**Harassment and Violence Prevention (SOR/2020-130):**
- Employers must have a workplace harassment and violence prevention policy
- Joint workplace assessment required to identify risk factors
- Resolution process: negotiated resolution → conciliation → investigation
- Final investigation report must be shared with parties (s. 56–60 of SOR/2020-130)

## Part III — Standard Hours, Wages, Vacations and Holidays

**Purpose:** Minimum employment standards for federally regulated employees (non-union baseline, but collective agreements may provide more).

### Hours of Work (Division I)

- **Standard hours:** 8 hours/day, 40 hours/week (s. 169)
- **Maximum hours:** 48 hours/week (s. 171) — with limited exceptions
- **Overtime:** Payable at 1.5× regular rate after 8 hours/day or 40 hours/week (s. 174)
- **Rest periods:** Minimum 30-minute break after every 5 consecutive hours of work (s. 169.1)
- **Hours free from work:** Minimum 8 consecutive hours between shifts (s. 169.2)
- **Compressed work weeks** and modified hours arrangements are permitted with agreement (s. 170, 172)

### Minimum Wages

- Federal minimum wage: reviewed annually — verify current rate at canada.ca/en/employment-social-development
- Applies to employees in federally regulated industries regardless of province
- Domestic workers have specific provisions under Part III

### Vacation (Division IV)

- **2 weeks** paid vacation after 1 year of continuous employment (s. 184)
- **3 weeks** after 5 consecutive years (s. 184)
- **4 weeks** after 10 consecutive years (Canada Labour Standards Regulations amendment)
- Vacation pay: 4% of earnings (2-week entitlement), 6% (3-week), 8% (4-week)

### General Holidays (Division V)

- 10 federal general holidays including New Year's Day, Good Friday, Victoria Day, Canada Day, Labour Day, National Day for Truth and Reconciliation, Thanksgiving, Remembrance Day, Christmas Day, and Boxing Day
- Employees who work on a general holiday are entitled to general holiday pay plus a holiday premium or a substitute day off (s. 196–206)

### Termination (Division VIII)

- **Statutory notice:** Employer must provide written notice of termination or pay in lieu (s. 230):
  - 1 week: employed 3 months to 1 year
  - 2 weeks: employed 1 to 3 years
  - 1 week per year of service: 3 to 8 years (max 8 weeks)
- **Severance pay:** Employees with 12+ months of continuous service terminated without cause may be entitled to 2 days' pay per year of service (minimum 5 days) (s. 235)
- **Unjust dismissal:** Employees with 12+ months of service not covered by a collective agreement can file an unjust dismissal complaint regardless of notice given — remedy may include reinstatement (s. 240–245)
- **Group termination:** If 50+ employees are terminated within a 4-week period, additional notice (up to 16 weeks) and a joint planning committee may be required (s. 212–214)
- **Constructive dismissal:** Unilateral and substantial changes to employment terms may constitute dismissal — high-risk, always escalate

### Leaves of Absence (Division VII)

Federal employees are entitled to the following unpaid protected leaves (employment protection during leave applies):

| Leave Type | Duration | Authority |
|------------|----------|-----------|
| Maternity leave | 17 weeks | s. 206 |
| Parental leave | 63 weeks (extended) or 40 weeks (standard) | s. 206.1 |
| Compassionate care leave | 28 weeks | s. 206.3 |
| Critical illness leave (child) | 37 weeks | s. 206.4 |
| Critical illness leave (adult) | 17 weeks | s. 206.4 |
| Death or disappearance leave | 104 weeks | s. 206.5 |
| Leave for victims of family violence | 10 days (3 paid after 3 months of service) + 17 weeks unpaid | s. 206.7 |
| Personal leave | 5 days (3 paid after 3 months of service) | s. 206.6 |
| Bereavement leave | 10 days (3 paid) | s. 210 |
| Court or jury duty | Duration of jury/witness service | s. 209 |
| Reserve force leave | 24 months | s. 247.5 |
| Medical leave | 17 weeks | s. 239 |

**Note:** Leave entitlements may be supplemented by EI benefits (administered by Service Canada — not part of the CLC).

## Part IV — Administrative Monetary Penalties

**Purpose:** Provides enforcement tools for Part II and Part III violations.

- Inspectors from ESDC may investigate and issue compliance orders
- Administrative monetary penalties (AMPs) may be assessed for contraventions
- Employers must cooperate with inspector investigations

---

## Canadian Human Rights Act (CHRA)

**Purpose:** Prohibits discrimination in employment (and services) on protected grounds in federally regulated sectors.

**Protected grounds:** Race, national or ethnic origin, colour, religion, age, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity or expression, marital status, family status, genetic characteristics, disability, and conviction for which a pardon has been granted.

**Key obligations:**
- Duty to accommodate to the point of undue hardship (s. 15(2))
- No discrimination in hiring, promotion, terms of employment, or termination
- Workplace harassment on a prohibited ground is a discriminatory practice (s. 14)
- Employers must respond promptly and appropriately to human rights complaints

**Enforcement:** Canadian Human Rights Commission (CHRC) → Canadian Human Rights Tribunal

## Employer Documentation Best Practices

| Situation | What to Document |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Hiring | Written offer letter, role description, compensation, start date, probation terms |
| Performance management | Written warnings with dates, employee acknowledgment |
| Termination | Decision record, notice calculation, severance calculation, final pay computation |
| Accommodation | Request received, interactive process steps, accommodation offered, undue hardship analysis if applicable |
| Harassment complaint | Complaint received date, investigation steps, findings, corrective action |
| Leaves | Leave request, confirmation of approval, return-to-work plan |
