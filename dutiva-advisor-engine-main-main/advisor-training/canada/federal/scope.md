# Federal Scope Definition

## In Scope (MVP)

- **Canada Labour Code (RSC 1985, c L-2)** — primary authority for federal employment standards
  - Part I: Industrial Relations (collective bargaining, unions)
  - Part II: Occupational Health and Safety (work refusal, safety committees, harassment/violence)
  - Part III: Standard Hours, Wages, Vacations and Holidays (hours of work, minimum wage, vacation, leaves, termination)
  - Part IV: Administrative Monetary Penalties
- **Canadian Human Rights Act (RSC 1985, c H-6)** — accommodation, discrimination, protected grounds
- **Work Place Harassment and Violence Prevention Regulations (SOR/2020-130)** — federal harassment/violence prevention process
- **Canada Labour Standards Regulations (C.R.C., c. 986)** — regulatory detail supporting CLC Part III

## Out of Scope (Phase 2+)

- Ontario Employment Standards Act, 2000
- Quebec Act Respecting Labour Standards
- BC Employment Standards Act
- Alberta Employment Standards Code
- Any other provincial employment standards act
- Tax law, immigration law, pension law, workers' compensation
- Common-law severance calculations (Bardal factors)

## Federally Regulated Sectors

An employer is federally regulated if its **core business** falls into one of these sectors:

| Sector | Examples |
|--------|---------|
| Banking | All Schedule I, II, III banks under the Bank Act |
| Airlines and air transport | Airlines, airports, air navigation |
| Telecommunications | Telephone, cable, internet providers, wireless carriers |
| Broadcasting | Television and radio broadcasters (licensed by CRTC) |
| Interprovincial rail | CN Rail, CP Rail, Via Rail |
| Interprovincial trucking | Carriers that cross provincial or international borders |
| Interprovincial pipelines | Oil and gas pipelines crossing provincial lines |
| Marine shipping and navigation | Shipping companies, navigation services |
| Grain handling | Grain elevators and inland terminals |
| Nuclear energy | Facilities regulated under the Nuclear Safety and Control Act |
| Federal Crown corporations | Canada Post, CBC/Radio-Canada, Export Development Canada, BDC, etc. |
| Port operations | Longshore workers and port authorities |
| Indigenous band councils | Some band council operations (requires legal confirmation) |

**Not federally regulated (usually):** retail, manufacturing, restaurants, hotels, construction, healthcare (hospitals/clinics), schools and universities, most provincial Crown corporations.

## Key Rules

### Jurisdiction First

Always confirm whether an employer is federally regulated **before** applying federal rules.

Do NOT assume federal jurisdiction based on:
- The employee working remotely
- The employer being incorporated federally
- The employer doing business across provinces
- What an employee handbook says

### Remote Work Is Not a Jurisdiction Trigger

If an employee of a federally regulated bank works from home in Ontario, the **Canada Labour Code still applies** — not the Ontario ESA. Jurisdiction follows the employer's regulated industry, not the employee's location.

If an employee of an Ontario retailer works remotely from BC, **Ontario ESA typically still applies**.

### When in Doubt

Flag the jurisdictional ambiguity explicitly. Do not guess. Direct the user to confirm with an employment lawyer.

## Advisor Behavior by Jurisdiction Signal

| Signal | Advisor action |
|--------|---------------|
| User confirms federal employer | Apply Canada Labour Code as primary authority |
| User confirms provincial employer | Acknowledge; note that current system covers federal only; refer to provincial Employment Standards ministry or HR professional |
| User has not stated jurisdiction | Ask: "Is your employer federally regulated (e.g., a bank, airline, or telecom company)?" |
| Jurisdiction ambiguous after asking | Flag the ambiguity; provide conditional federal guidance; recommend legal confirmation |

## Human Rights Jurisdiction

Human rights law is also jurisdiction-split:

| Employer type | Applicable human rights law | Enforcement body |
|--------------|---------------------------|-----------------|
| Federally regulated | Canadian Human Rights Act (CHRA) | Canadian Human Rights Commission (CHRC) |
| Provincially regulated (Ontario) | Ontario Human Rights Code | Human Rights Tribunal of Ontario |
| Provincially regulated (Quebec) | Charter of Human Rights and Freedoms | Commission des droits de la personne |
| All other provinces | Provincial human rights code | Provincial body |

The Advisor (MVP) applies the **CHRA only**. Provincial human rights codes are Phase 2.
